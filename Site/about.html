About

After six years’ experience as an educator, I decided to make a career change and learn web development. As a teacher, I excelled at organization and breaking problems down to make plans for each step. I also gained clear and positive communication skills, both with students and colleagues. However, I found myself wanting new challenges and a change in lifestyle. A couple years ago, a family member suggested I begin learning coding, and I haven’t looked back!


I began my journey with coding through the many amazing resources that are available online. After a year or so of this, I craved a more formal program to help me connect with like-minded people who were starting out in this industry. I chose Anyone Can Learn to Code, a “coding bootcamp” that meets in 1871 in Chicago. This is an immersive 15-week web development training program. I absolutely loved the chance to not only learn different web frameworks, but also to collaborate with others and learn from their experiences.


During and since my time at Anyone Can Learn to Code, I’ve created or contributed to a number of projects using these technologies (to name a few):

  • Ruby on Rails
  • JavaScript
  • AngularJS
  • HTML, CSS, Bootstrap

More than anything, I love working with others. This provides so much opportunity for learning and growth. I hope to find a position in an environment where my colleagues value being part of a team as much as I do.


In my free time I enjoy cooking, playing piano, and listening to podcasts. I volunteer at the Art Institute of Chicago every other Saturday afternoon, where I love meeting people from all over the world. I also love to travel, hike, and play board games!
